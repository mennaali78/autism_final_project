What is autism?

Autism, or autism spectrum disorder (ASD), refers to a broad range of conditions characterized by challenges with social skills, repetitive behaviors, speech and nonverbal communication.

Environmental factors 
  • Advanced parental age
  • Prenatal exposure to air pollution or certain pesticides
  • Maternal obesity, diabetes or immune system disorders
  • Extreme prematurity or very low birth weight
  • Birth complications leading to periods of oxygen deprivation to the baby’s brain

Early Signs of Autism

Repetitive Behaviors

Repeats the same movements like hand flapping or lining up toys.

Sensory Sensitivities

Strong reaction to sounds, lights, or certain textures.

Lack of Pointing

Does not point to show interest or share attention.

Delayed Speech

Speech develops later than children of the same age.

Avoiding Eye Contact

Rarely makes eye contact during interaction.

Limited Interest in Peers

Prefers to play alone rather than with other children.

Difficulty with Routines

Gets upset when daily routines change.

expression that do not fit the situation

Reacting with emotions that seem unusual for the moment.
